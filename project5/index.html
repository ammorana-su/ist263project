    • The Start of the Journey

    My passion for weight lifting started the summer before my senior year of high school. Since then, I've put in over 5 years of consistent work, hitting the gym at least 6 days a week without fail. When I first started, I was all about raw strength. I wanted to see how much weight I could move. Ego-lifting as they call in the bodybuilding world.

    The "Natural Responder" vs. The "Hard Gainer"

    It didn't take long for me to realize I was what people call a "natural responder." My muscles responded to weight training almost immediately. In just my first 6 months, I went from 140 to 150 lbs, and most of that was lean muscle. Even though I gained muscle quickly, I realized I'm naturally a "hard gainer" with a crazy high metabolism. I have to be extremely intentional with my calories, or the weight just falls off.

    I also found out I was genetically geared for strength-I managed to get my bench up to 255 lbs in the first 7 months while only weighing 150 lbs. That officially put me in the top 1% for my weight class, which was a huge ego boost and really solidified my love for the grind.

    Current Stats & Beyond

    Even though I focus on hypertrophy now, those strength roots are still there. My current maxes for the "Big Three" are:

    • Squat: 500 lbs
    • Bench: 325 lbs
    • Deadlift: 505 lbs

    Through this journey, I've learned that lifting is only half the battle. To really maximize your potential, you have to look "under the hood" at your internal health and your metabolic rate, which I'll dive into on the next pages.
